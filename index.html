Designing Design into Society

Project: Ernest Beck

Project Dose

What do you do when you see a nurse in Uganda crushing tablets in a hospital with a Coca-Cola bottle in order to create children's dosages? If you are Linda Pulik you create Project Dose, an initiative (and winner of a Sappi "Ideas That Matter" grant) that aims to eliminate the health risks associated with improper medication delivery with an affordable, paper-based product that lets nurses, pharmacists and parents safely divide and repackage adult tables into smaller doses for children.

Essay: Andrew Shea

Flies in Urinals: The Value of Design Disruptions

Many are dubious of the recent surge of design altruism, noting that they rarely see evidence of social impact projects that lead to real behavior change. While it is well documented that public awareness campaigns generally work for actions that people perform infrequently, it is much harder to change habitual actions in a meaningful way. Since daily habits are profoundly shaped by our environment, perhaps designers must disrupt the environment itself to change behaviors?

Essay: William Drenttel

Designing for Social Change

Designing for Social Change is a toolkit of strategies, case studies, and stories, offering new opportunities for approaching social design in our communities. It presents students and schools as active participants, designers and design firms as social innovators, and communities as both rich laboratories for experimentation and receptive locations for creative approaches and new ideas.

DART St. Louis
